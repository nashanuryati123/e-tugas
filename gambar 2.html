Psychosocial Condition of the Subject

Throughout her childhood and adolescence, the subject showed a tendency to suppress emotions and was unaccustomed to expressing her feelings to family members. While her relationships with both her father and mother were generally superficially good, the subject did not feel she had a safe space to share her feelings, especially regarding her confusion or disappointment about the family dynamics.

Upon entering adolescence, the subject began to experience an increased emotional awareness of the situation she had lived with since childhood. Feelings of confusion, disappointment, and emotional exhaustion became more apparent, as her ability to comprehend her parents' relational dynamics grew. Although she did not exhibit explicit behavioral problems, the internal pressure from this inner conflict began to influence her perspective on relationships and her future.
